Effects of Pollution on Young Generations

Good Morning to our respected principal mam, V.P mam, co-ordinator mam, Incharge mam, teachers, and my beloved friends. I, Anushree Raginee, am here in front of you with a very engaging topic, "The Effect of Pollution on Young Generation."

According to the United Nations Children's Fund, hazardous air is inhaled by 17 million babies around the world. Because children are still developing, air pollution damages them during this stage of their lives, resulting in long-term health concerns. Air pollution exposure at a young age can stifle lung growth, stunt brain development, and raise the likelihood of developing asthma. Certain ailments, including as lung cancer, heart disease, and acute respiratory infections, have been linked to air pollution. As we all know, children and kids are the future of our generation, so it is up to us to think about and rescue our future. Let us band together to rescue our future by taking simple and practical initiatives. For example, municipalities can create clean air zones for children walking to school and reduce automobile traffic along specific routes. Switching to renewable energy sources and drastically cutting emissions in the transportation industry can help to reduce air pollution globally. Making these changes now is critical to preventing irreparable health conditions from limiting our children's futures.

Thank you for patiently listening to my speech. I hope you enjoyed the speech. Have a cheerful day ahead!
